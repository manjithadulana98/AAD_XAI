SECTION 7: CROSS-METHOD SYNTHESIS — KEY FINDINGS
==============================================================================

  FINDING 1: DIFFERENT SPATIAL STRATEGIES
  VLAAI uses suppressive Central/Occipital channels as negative evidence.
  TRF relies on Frontal/Temporal channels for envelope tracking.
  Channel rank correlation ≈ 0 → fundamentally different spatial features.
  Implication: The deep model discovers inhibitory patterns that a linear
  model cannot capture.

  FINDING 2: DIVERGENT FREQUENCY UTILIZATION
  Both models use delta (1-4 Hz) as the primary informative band.
  However, VLAAI uniquely exploits theta as suppressive (negative) evidence.
  TRF cannot learn such nonlinear frequency interactions.
  The shared delta reliance confirms cortical envelope tracking as the
  core mechanism for both models.

  FINDING 3: TEMPORAL SENSITIVITY REFLECTS ARCHITECTURE
  VLAAI shows early-segment sensitivity (causal architecture with memory).
  TRF shows near-uniform temporal sensitivity (no temporal memory).
  VLAAI's sensitivity magnitude is ~3-5x higher than TRF's,
  indicating stronger temporal feature encoding.

  FINDING 4: SUBJECT-WISE ADAPTATION vs CONSISTENCY
  TRF is more consistent across subjects (r=0.10 vs r=-0.01).
  VLAAI shows subject-specific spatial patterns (Fronto-Central vs Parietal).
  This reflects a fundamental trade-off: linear models are constrained,
  deep models are adaptive but harder to interpret.

  FINDING 5: ACCURACY WITH PROPER TRAINING
  Within-subject TRF (68.9% at 5s) surpasses both VLAAI (65.0%) and
  literature baselines (57.5%). At longer windows (40s), TRF reaches 88.9%.
  Key insight: a well-tuned linear model can OUTPERFORM a pretrained deep
  model when matched for training paradigm (within-subject).

  FINDING 6: CORRECT PREDICTIONS USE DIFFERENT NETWORKS
  VLAAI correct predictions rely on Parietal (attention network).
  VLAAI incorrect predictions rely on Frontal (possible artifacts).
  This suggests VLAAI's errors stem from attending to non-auditory signals,
  while its successes leverage neuroscientifically grounded features.

  FINDING 7: EXPLAINABILITY-ACCURACY TRADE-OFF
  TRF is inherently interpretable (linear weights ≈ temporal response function).
  VLAAI requires post-hoc XAI methods (occlusion, GradCAM, IG).
  However, VLAAI's XAI reveals richer patterns (suppression, band interactions,
  subject-specific adaptation) that TRF cannot express.
  For clinical deployment, TRF's transparency may be preferred despite
  lower accuracy; for research, VLAAI's complex patterns are more informative.
